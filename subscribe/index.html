Coverage of the JIT access, PAM, and privileged access management market written for practitioners who are already in the work. No vendor announcements repackaged as analysis. No rankings paid for with sponsored placements.

What it covers

The Independent Defender covers the JIT access market with the same editorial standards as the rest of this site. That means practitioner register throughout, vendor-neutral framing, and a willingness to say when a product category is being oversold relative to what it delivers in production environments.

Each issue covers one or more of the following: architectural developments in the JIT and PAM market, operational problems that practitioners report encountering after deployment, significant vendor moves (acquisitions, major product changes, pricing shifts), and the JIT/NHI convergence space where service account governance and ephemeral workload credentials are being addressed from two different category names.

The Defender is part of a newsletter family covering the broader cybersecurity software market. Sibling issues cover non-human identity governance (nonhumanidentitysoftware.com), data security posture management (dspmsoftware.com), and attack surface management (attacksurfacemanagementsoftware.com). A single subscription covers all of them.
